HEADACHE (Cefalea) - Triage and Management

Primary headaches account for over 90% of cases. The main types are tension-type headache, migraine, and cluster headache.

Tension-type headache is the most common. Symptoms include bilateral pressing or tightening pain, mild to moderate intensity, not aggravated by routine physical activity. Duration ranges from 30 minutes to 7 days. First-line treatment: paracetamol 500-1000mg or ibuprofene 200-400mg. Do not exceed recommended daily doses.

Migraine presents as unilateral pulsating pain, moderate to severe intensity, aggravated by physical activity, often with nausea, photophobia, or phonophobia. Duration 4-72 hours. Aura may precede the headache in some patients. First-line treatment: triptani (e.g. sumatriptan) as prescribed by doctor, or FANS (ibuprofene, naprossene). Preventive therapy may be needed if attacks occur more than 4 times per month.

RED FLAGS requiring immediate emergency evaluation (call 112):
- Thunderclap headache: worst headache of life, sudden onset reaching maximum intensity in seconds
- Headache with fever, neck stiffness, rash (possible meningitis)
- Headache with neurological symptoms: confusion, weakness, vision loss, speech difficulty
- Headache after head trauma
- New headache in patient over 50 years old with temporal tenderness (possible giant cell arteritis)
- Headache with papilledema or progressive worsening over weeks

FEVER (Febbre) - Management Guidelines

Normal body temperature: 36.1-37.2 C (oral). Fever is defined as temperature above 38.0 C.

Low-grade fever (37.5-38.0): Monitor, increase fluid intake, rest. Generally does not require antipyretics.
Moderate fever (38.0-39.0): Paracetamol or ibuprofene for comfort. Increase fluids significantly. Rest.
High fever (39.0-40.0): Antipyretics recommended. Cool compresses. Seek medical attention if persists more than 3 days.
Very high fever (above 40.0): Seek immediate medical attention.

In children: Fever above 38.0 in infants under 3 months requires immediate medical evaluation. For children 3 months to 3 years, seek medical attention if fever above 39.0 or persists more than 24 hours. Always monitor for dehydration signs.

NEVER use aspirin (acido acetilsalicilico) in children under 16 due to risk of Reye syndrome.

COMMON MEDICATIONS - AIFA Reference

Paracetamolo (Tachipirina):
- Use: Pain relief, fever reduction
- NOT an anti-inflammatory
- Generally well-tolerated at recommended doses
- DANGER: Hepatotoxicity (liver damage) at high doses. Maximum 3g per day for adults. Never combine with alcohol.
- Available over-the-counter

Ibuprofene (Moment, Brufen):
- Use: Pain relief, fever reduction, anti-inflammatory
- Take with food to protect stomach
- Common side effects: GI upset, heartburn, nausea
- Avoid in: Last trimester of pregnancy, active stomach ulcers, severe kidney or liver disease
- Caution with: Anticoagulants, other FANS, ACE inhibitors
- Maximum 1200mg per day (OTC) without prescription

Amoxicillina:
- Antibiotic, penicillin class
- Use: Bacterial infections (ear, throat, urinary, respiratory)
- ALWAYS complete the full prescribed course
- Common side effects: Diarrhea, nausea, rash
- Allergic reactions possible: if rash, swelling, difficulty breathing -> emergency

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR (Guida alla Priorita)

See within 24 hours:
- Fever above 39 persisting more than 2 days
- Ear pain with discharge
- Urinary symptoms (burning, frequency, blood)
- Persistent vomiting or diarrhea beyond 24 hours
- Skin infection showing spreading redness

See within the week:
- Cough lasting more than 2 weeks
- Recurring headaches
- Unexplained weight loss
- Persistent fatigue
- Joint pain or swelling
- Skin lesion that is changing

EMERGENCY - Call 112 immediately:
- Chest pain or pressure
- Difficulty breathing
- Sudden weakness on one side (stroke signs: FAST - Face, Arms, Speech, Time)
- Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)
- Severe bleeding that does not stop with pressure
- Loss of consciousness
- Seizures
- Severe abdominal pain
- Signs of meningitis (fever, stiff neck, rash that does not blanch)
