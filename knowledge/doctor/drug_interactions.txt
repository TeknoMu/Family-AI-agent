COMMON DRUG INTERACTIONS - Family Reference

FANS (Anti-inflammatory drugs) interactions:
- Ibuprofene + Aspirina: Ibuprofene can reduce the cardioprotective effect of low-dose aspirin. Take aspirin at least 30 minutes before ibuprofene if both are needed.
- Ibuprofene + Warfarin/anticoagulanti: Increased bleeding risk. Avoid combination or use with strict medical supervision.
- Ibuprofene + ACE-inibitori (ramipril, enalapril): Can reduce blood pressure medication effectiveness and increase kidney risk.
- Ibuprofene + Litio: Can increase lithium blood levels to toxic range. Avoid.
- Ibuprofene + Metotrexato: Can increase methotrexate toxicity. Avoid.
- Multiple FANS together: NEVER combine two FANS (e.g. ibuprofene + naprossene + ketoprofene). Greatly increases stomach and kidney risk with no additional benefit.

Paracetamolo interactions:
- Paracetamolo + Alcol: Regular alcohol use with paracetamol significantly increases liver damage risk. Limit to maximum 2g/day paracetamol if drinking.
- Paracetamolo + Warfarin: Can increase INR (bleeding risk). Monitor more frequently.
- Paracetamolo is generally the safest analgesic for patients on most medications.

Antibiotici interactions:
- Amoxicillina + contraccettivi orali: Historically thought to reduce contraceptive effectiveness; current evidence suggests this is minimal except with rifampicina.
- Ciprofloxacina + dairy products/antacids: Calcium, magnesium, aluminum reduce absorption. Take ciprofloxacin 2 hours before or 6 hours after.
- Metronidazolo + Alcol: Severe reaction (nausea, vomiting, flushing, headache). Avoid alcohol during treatment and 48 hours after.
- Antibiotics + probiotics: Take probiotics at least 2 hours apart from antibiotics to maintain effectiveness.

Statine (cholesterol drugs) interactions:
- Atorvastatina/Simvastatina + Succo di pompelmo: Grapefruit can dramatically increase statin blood levels, increasing risk of muscle damage (rhabdomyolysis). Avoid grapefruit.
- Statine + Fibrati (gemfibrozil): Increased rhabdomyolysis risk. Fenofibrato is safer to combine.
- Statine + Macrolidi (eritromicina, claritromicina): Increased statin levels. Azitromicina is safer.

FOOD AND MEDICATION TIMING

Take WITH food: Ibuprofene, naprossene, ketoprofene (all FANS), metformina, prednisolone.
Take on EMPTY stomach (1 hour before or 2 hours after eating): Levotiroxina (thyroid), ciprofloxacina, omeprazolo (30 min before breakfast).
Avoid dairy within 2 hours: Tetracicline, ciprofloxacina, supplementi di ferro.
Avoid alcohol: Metronidazolo, benzodiazepine, antistaminici, paracetamolo (limit).

COMMON SUPPLEMENTS AND SAFETY

Vitamina D: Recommended in Italy especially during winter. Typical dose 1000-2000 UI/day for adults. Safe up to 4000 UI/day. Higher doses require medical supervision and blood level monitoring.

Ferro (Iron): Take on empty stomach with vitamin C for better absorption. Common side effects: constipation, dark stools, nausea. Do not take with calcium, dairy, tea, or coffee (reduce absorption by 50-70%).

Magnesio: Helps with muscle cramps, sleep, stress. Well-tolerated. High doses may cause diarrhea. Safe for most adults at 200-400mg/day.

Probiotici: Support gut health especially during/after antibiotic therapy. Take at least 2 hours apart from antibiotics. Generally very safe. Look for multi-strain formulas with at least 10 billion CFU.

VACCINATION SCHEDULE - Adult Reference (Italy)

Annual: Influenza vaccine (recommended for all, especially over 65 and chronic conditions)
Every 10 years: Tetanus-diphtheria booster (richiamo tetano-difterite)
Once (if not done): Varicella (if no history of disease), MMR (morbillo-parotite-rosolia)
Over 50: Herpes Zoster vaccine (fuoco di Sant Antonio) recommended
Over 65: Pneumococcal vaccine recommended
